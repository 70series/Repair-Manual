CALIBRATION


NOTICE:
·

After replacing the skid control ECU (brake actuator assembly), perform "Calibration".

·

If the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) was replaced or reinstalled, perform "Calibration".

·

Perform "Calibration" with the vehicle on a level surface (within 0.25° gradient). Do not vibrate the vehicle or open or close doors during zero point calibration. To avoid vibrations due to idling, do not start the engine.

·

Perform "Calibration" with the tires at the specified air pressures and all 4 wheels in contact with the ground. (Do not perform with the vehicle lifted up or load applied on lift.)



HINT:
·

When entering dealer mode (calibration mode), the skid control ECU communicates with the forward recognition camera to judge whether the vehicle is equipped with the pre-collision system. Therefore, do not exit dealer mode (calibration mode) within 5 seconds of entering dealer mode (calibration mode).

·

Performing the following procedure will perform the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) zero point calibration and store system information simultaneously.

·

If yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) and zero point calibration do not complete normally, the slip indicator light illuminates and a DTC is output.



a.

Check that the shift lever is in neutral.


b.

Turn the ignition switch to ON.


c.

Check that the steering wheel is centered.


d.

Switch the skid control ECU (brake actuator assembly) to Calibration using the GTS.

Chassis > Brake > Utility
Tester Display
Calibration
50005 435 4554

e.

Check that the ABS warning light and slip indicator light are in dealer mode (calibration mode) display (blinking at 0.25-second intervals).

NOTICE:

When dealer mode (calibration mode) is selected with the parking brake is not applied, the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) zero point calibration incomplete DTC is stored.


HINT:
·

When "Calibration" completes normally, the ABS warning light and slip indicator light are in dealer mode (calibration mode) display (blinking at 0.25-second intervals).

·

The slip indicator light may illuminate for approximately 7 seconds until "Calibration" completes normally.

·

Do not exit dealer mode (calibration mode) within 5 seconds of entering dealer mode (calibration mode). If dealer mode (calibration mode) is exited within 5 seconds of entering dealer mode (calibration mode), DTC U023A87 may be stored. If DTC U023A87 is output after exiting test mode, enter test mode again, wait at least 5 seconds, then recheck for DTCs.




f.

Turn the ignition switch off.



RESET MEMORY


NOTICE:

After performing "Reset Memory", make sure to perform "Calibration".


a.

Check that the shift lever is in neutral.


b.

Turn the ignition switch off.


c.

Connect the GTS to the DLC3.


d.

Turn the ignition switch to ON.


e.

Turn the GTS on.


f.

Select the skid control ECU (brake actuator assembly) to Reset Memory using the GTS. Enter the following menus: Chassis / Brake / Utility / Reset Memory.

Chassis > Brake > Utility
Tester Display
Reset Memory
50005 435 4560
NOTICE:
·

If the ignition switch is turned to ON for more than 15 seconds with the parking brake applied after the zero point of the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) has been cleared, only the zero point of the yaw rate sensor will be stored. If the vehicle is driven under these conditions, the skid control ECU (brake actuator assembly) will store the zero point calibration for the acceleration sensor as not being completed. The skid control ECU (brake actuator assembly) will then also indicate this as a malfunction of the vehicle stability control system using the indicator light.

·

When the vehicle is driven with the zero point cleared, the yaw rate and acceleration sensor (airbag ECU assembly) zero point calibration incomplete DTC is stored.




g.

Turn the ignition switch off.
